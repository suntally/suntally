# SunTally — talk tracks

Reusable scripts for posts, comments, DMs, and conversations. Same voice everywhere:
honest, numerate, anti-hype. We never sell solar — we help people see the truth.

---

## The 15-second hook
> "Your solar installer gave you one payback number at signing. SunTally shows you
> the real one — from your own utility bills, in your browser, for free. No account, no
> upload, nothing leaves your device."

## The origin story (most powerful — use for Show HN / longform)
> "I put solar on my house in 2019 and couldn't get a straight answer to 'is this
> actually paying off?' The utility bill hides it: solar offsets generation charges but
> not delivery and fixed fees. So I tracked every bill for 7 years in a spreadsheet —
> true cost, true ROI, how much I pay the utility as a delivery company vs. a generation
> company. This is that spreadsheet, turned into a free open-source tool anyone can use
> with their own bills."

## The honesty angle
> "It doesn't try to sell you solar. Plug in your real numbers and it'll happily tell you
> your system is underwater — mine was, for stretches. That's the point: an honest tool,
> not a sales calculator."

## The privacy angle
> "There's no backend. Your bills are parsed and stored in your browser, full stop. It's
> open source — read every line and confirm it. The reason it can stay free is that it
> costs almost nothing to run."

## The 'delivery fees' insight (great for Reddit screenshots)
> "The chart that surprised people most: for many homes, the majority of the electric
> bill is delivery and fixed fees, not the energy itself. Solar can't touch that part —
> which is exactly why the bill never goes to zero. SunTally shows you your split."

## The donation angle (place AFTER value is delivered, never before)
> "SunTally is free and will never charge users or sell data. If it saved you from a
> bad assumption, you can tip the project. And if you're a solar company that wants honest
> tools in your customers' hands, you can sponsor a release — there's a tasteful credit
> slot, no tracking."

## For installers / B2B (sponsorship pitch)
> "Most homeowners distrust solar math because every calculator is a sales tool. A neutral,
> open-source one you can hand to leads sets honest expectations and builds trust — and you
> get a clean 'sponsored by' credit. Want to back a release?"

- "Solar math without the salesperson."

## FAQ-style rebuttals
- *"Isn't this just a calculator?"* — Two things at once: a forward projection for buyers,
  and a real dashboard built from your actual bills for owners. The second is the hard part.
- *"Why should I trust the numbers?"* — Because they're yours, the assumptions are visible
  and editable, and the source is open.
- *"What's the catch / how do you make money?"* — No catch. It's donation- and
  sponsor-funded, and stays free. Costs are near zero because there's no server.
